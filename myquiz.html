Context of the Poem

The “Lucy” poems are a series of five poems written by William Wordsworth between 1798 and 1801. These poems explore themes of nature, death, love, and loss, often focusing on a mysterious young woman named Lucy. The poems are celebrated for their simplicity, emotional depth, and use of natural imagery.
